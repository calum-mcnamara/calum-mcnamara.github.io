Calum McNamara

I'm an Assistant Professor of Philosophy at Indiana University, Bloomington. Before coming to IU, I was a post-doc at Yale. I received my PhD from the University of Michigan. I work mostly on decision theory, epistemology, and the philosophy of AI. I also have interests in ethics, metaphysics, philosophy of language, and philosophy of science.

Papers

Causal Decision Theory, Context, and Determinism
Philosophy and Phenomenological Research, 2024
Synopsis: I respond to "deterministic" counterexamples to causal decision theory by drawing attention to the fact that counterfactuals are context-sensitive.
